Exercise: Algorithmic Efficiency

Question 1

This algorithm is O(n). What does ’n’ represent in this example?

n represents the number of elements in the array, given as the exampleSize variable.

Question 2

What is this algorithm’s time complexity, in terms of 'n'?

This algorithm's complexity is O(n2), where n is the number of rows and columns. We can determine this by examining the loops. The outer loop runs n times (i < n) and the inner loop also runs n times (j < n). This means that in total there will be n * n = n2 iterations.

Question 3

What is the total time complexity of Sally’s task, in terms of ’n’ and ‘m'?

Selection sort has a efficiency of O(n2). Since notifying 1 person has an efficiency of O(1), notifying m people will have an efficiency of O(m). The total efficiency will be the efficiency of the sort combined with the efficiency of notifying everyone, or O(n2 + m).
